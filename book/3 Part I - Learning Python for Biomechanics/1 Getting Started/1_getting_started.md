# Getting Started

Depending on your background, you may want to start your journey at different points:

## I never programmed using Python.

Simply start at the beginning: [](2_getting_started_installing.md).


## I know Python, NumPy, and Matplotlib, but I'm new to biomechanical analysis.

- You may be interested in a refresher by attempting these different [programming exercises](../2%20Learning%20Python/python_exercises.md).
- Then, jump to [](../../4%20Part%20II%20-%20Going%20Further%20with%20Kinetics%20Toolkit/ktk_importing.md).


## I already do biomechanical analysis, but under Matlab instead of Python.

- You may want to start at the beginning to learn the Python syntax: [](2_getting_started_installing.md).
- You may skip [](../3%20Creating%20figures%20with%20Matplotlib/1_matplotlib.md) as this is practically identical to Matlab.
- However, be sure to go through [](../4%20Manipulating%20Arrays%20using%20Numpy/1%20numpy.md) as there are some fundamental differences between Matlab and NumPy.
- Then, jump to [](../../4%20Part%20II%20-%20Going%20Further%20with%20Kinetics%20Toolkit/ktk_importing.md).

## I already do biomechanical analysis under Python.

You may want to understand what Kinetics Toolkit could add to your Python workflow. Start directly here: [](../../4%20Part%20II%20-%20Going%20Further%20with%20Kinetics%20Toolkit/ktk_importing.md).

You may also consult the [API Reference](https://kineticstoolkit.uqam.ca/docs/api).
